How it's made

From wall
to wrist.

Each piece of Urban Fragments jewellery begins with a wall that is about to disappear.

Placeholder copy — client to provide process descriptions
01

Document

We identify walls across European cities that are scheduled for demolition or repainting. Before a single fragment is removed, we document the full surface — photography, artist details where known, location and date. Every wall has a record.

02

Reclaim

Working with property owners and, where possible, the original artists, we carefully remove sections of the painted surface. The paint layer is preserved in its original state. Nothing is added, cleaned, or altered.

03

Cut & set

Back in the studio, each fragment is evaluated individually. We cut it to shape, taking care to preserve the most expressive areas of paint. The piece is then set by hand into sterling silver — sometimes gold-plated — to create a wearable form. No two cuts are the same.

04

Document & certificate

Before leaving the studio, each piece is photographed and catalogued. Every item ships with a certificate of origin: the wall it came from, the city, the year, and the artist's name where we have it. You're not just buying jewellery — you're buying a documented fragment of urban history.

The material

Real paint.
Real walls.

Placeholder copy — client to provide

The fragments we work with are not reproductions. They are sections cut from actual painted concrete and brick walls — surfaces that were once visible to anyone walking past. The colours, textures, and layering you see on the jewellery are exactly as they appeared on the wall. We don't touch them. We don't add to them. The material does the work.

Why it matters

Art that would
otherwise
disappear.

Cities change. Murals get painted over. Buildings come down. Most street art is temporary by nature — appreciated in passing, then gone. Urban Fragments is a small act of preservation. We can't save every wall. But we can save a piece of it.
