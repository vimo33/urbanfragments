The idea

"We walk past painted walls every day without thinking about them. When a building comes down, that art disappears forever. We cut it into jewellery instead."

Urban Fragments is built on a simple act of preservation. Across European cities — from Berlin's last remaining Hinterhof murals to the layered walls of Lisbon's Mouraria — we document, negotiate access, and hand-cut sections of painted concrete before demolition begins. Each fragment becomes a single piece of jewellery. Nothing is replicated. Once it's gone, it's gone.

How it works

From
wall to wrist.

01

Document

We photograph and map every wall before a single cut is made — building a permanent archive of what existed before the city moved on.

02

Reclaim

Working with building owners and demolition contractors, we extract sections of painted concrete while the wall is still standing. The material is irreplaceable.

03

Set by hand

Each fragment is cut, shaped, and set individually in oxidised silver. No two settings are identical — the stone dictates the form.

04

Certificated

Every piece ships with a certificate of origin documenting the wall, city, year of reclamation, and the artist (where known).
